How it works?
Input the simulation parameters and then click on "Start Simulation" to observe the results on the graphs below.
Input parameters:
Simulation Name: Name for the current simulation. This name can be used later on to retrieve past simulation results.
Mortality Rate per day: Pecentage of the total population that dies during one day. This number should be less that 100.
Infection Rate per day: Pecentage of the total population gets infected during one day. This number should be less that 100
Population Size: Amount of people that make up the simulation's population.
Susceptible %: Percentage of the total population that will be initially considered as susceptible to the disease.
Infected %: Percentage of the total population that will be initially considered as infected by the disease.
Incoubating %: Percentage of the total population that will be initially considered as incoubating.
Treated %: Percentage of the total population that will be initially considered as treated against the disease.
Cured %: Percentage of the total population that will be initially considered as cured from the disease.
Note: Only "Simulation Name", "Population Size", "Mortality Rate" and "Infection Rate" are mandatory parameters.
If no percentage is specified, 99% of the population will be considered as susceptible and the remaining 1% will be considered as infected.
If at least one percentage is specified, the total sum must be 100 (empty fields will be considered as 0%)
